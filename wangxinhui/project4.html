SQUARES & STREETS

The square is a wide and defined enclosed space, and it is also a road node and a pedestrian stop. The street is mainly used as a circulation line and also as a route to the square.

I analyzed St. Mark's Square, Shell Square, and Ancient Roman Market Square in Italy to see the relationship between virtual and real spaces.

RESIDENT RELATIONS

This is a collective housing project. I use the commune to increase the interaction of residents, and the square serves as a node to gather people and attract non-residents to stay. So I use the 1st and 4th floors as public facilities. The 1st floor is more welcome for non-residents, while the 4th floor is mainly for residents.

RESIDENTIAL UNITS

There are three room types of 33 square meters, 66 square meters and 99 square meters. The smallest units are mainly for single people, couples or couples. The 66-square-meter unit is mainly for small families or renters. The largest room type is mainly for families.

PLAN

The main living units are distributed on both sides, with holes dug in the middle, and the square on the first floor can be seen from the glass floor-to-ceiling windows. Floors 1.5.8 are public areas for residents, where classrooms, nurseries, gyms and roof gardens can be used.
