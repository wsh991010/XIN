SITE INTRODUCTION

The base is located next to the Yongle Market in Dadaocheng, Taipei City, which is full of cultural atmosphere, and many residents and foreign tourists will buy fabrics here.

Nearby are interlocking shops and residences, which retain the architectural style of the Japanese occupation period.

ANALYSIS

The historical evolution of local architectural forms has changed from traditional southern Fujian-style buildings to Western-style buildings during the Japanese colonial period. One of the important features is the form of round arches. Common arched facade forms include three-arch and single-arch forms.

I analyzed the corresponding relationship between real space and virtual space, and designed the plan and elevations.

PROGRAM

This design program is a cultural and creative center, which must include studios, shops, classrooms, factories, exhibition spaces and performance centers. I use the properties of the base to configure the floor plan.

The west side of the base is the Yongle Market, and more people will enter from this side, so I designed a more layered facade. On the right side is facing the main road, there are more cars, so I made a continuous facade with the neighboring house.

PLAN

The first and second floors of the basement are parking lots. I have arranged a total of 28 general parking spaces, 16 locomotive parking spaces, and 15 unloading parking spaces. I arranged the elevator room on the east side. The first floor is a relatively open place. I made a semi-open shopping street, with two nodes where you can stop and rest, and designed some shade, so you can go out to the other side while shopping.

The second and third floors are divided into three buildings, which can be accessed by different stairs. The main space is the factory and the lecture and exhibition space. The fourth floor is a large platform connecting all buildings. People can walk from the building to the outside platform to bask in the sun and to another building.

The 5th to 8th floors are mainly classroom space, and the 9th to 10th floors are administrative spaces.
